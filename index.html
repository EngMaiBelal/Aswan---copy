Luxor
and
Aswan:
Two cities of ancient wonders

Luxor, which was once known as Thebes (the capital of Ancient Egypt) is located in Southern Egypt- 670 km South of Cairo. Luxor today is a city of around 150,000 people. This city is prominent for being an “open air museum” because of its unique archaeological monuments. Luxor is the second most visited tourist city in Egypt after Cairo. Extravagant monuments such as Karnak Temple, The Luxor Temple, Valley of Kings, Valley of Queens and many more are the reason for this.

The Karnak Temple Complex

The Karnak Temple Complex (which was known as Ipet-isut during the time of the Pharaohs) is the most famous temple in Luxor. It was built by the pharaohs for over 2000 years and was dedicated to the Pharaoh Amun.

The Luxor Temple

The Luxor Templeis amongst one of the beauties of Luxor. It is a large ancient temple complex located on the East bank of the Nile River.

Amanhotep II and Ramses II

Amanhotep II and Ramses II built it in 14000 B.C. Built with sandstone, this temple’s main purpose was to celebrate the Festival of Opet.

The Festival of Opet

This festival was by far the largest and most important celebration by the Pharaohs. I was held in honor of the great Pharaoh Amun in the season of inundation.

Nile River cruise

Another activity carried by most tourists visiting the city of Luxor is going on a Nile boat cruise from Luxor to Aswan.
Enjoy a lavish Nile River cruise and discover some of Egypt’s most enchanting natural beauty.
Aswan, (previously known as Syene during Ancient Egypt) is located about 81 KM south of Luxor.
This small modern city has a population of about 230,000 people who are mostly Nubians. (People of an ancient region of Northeastern Africa)
